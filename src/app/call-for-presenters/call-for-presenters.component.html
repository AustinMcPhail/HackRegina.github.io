Call for Presenters

Speaking at HackRegina Events

Would you like to speak at a HackRegina event? We welcome you! Of course, we have guidelines that we ask all of our presenters to follow. These guidelines ensure that our members receive value from every presentation, that the speakers receive value through interaction with our members and that our sponsors are well represented.

Speaker Guidelines

  1. You must LOVE tech as much as we do. This is the perfect opportunity to preach to the choir. We are a general tech community and welcome talks on a broad range of topics from deep dives in to a specific tech, to experiences with a new language or framework, to startup life and culture.
  2. No sales pitches please. HackRegina is an informal group of professionals who get together every month because we want to, not as part of our jobs or career development. The meeting is definitely not meant as a means to be sold new products.
  3. Attend some of our meetups prior to presenting. This is arguably the best way to get a sense for the community you’ll be addressing. If you can’t make it to a meetup, try joining the HackRegina Slack.
  4. We would like to record as many of our presentations on video if possible. You’re welcome to use the video on your own blog or web site.

Typical Presentation Formats

Tech Talk

Tech talks are meant for the community to share their knowledge to help Regina stay ahead of new and popular technologies for developing applications, software solutions and developing new skills.

Agenda:

  • [30 minutes] Networking + eating refreshments (if provided).
  • [45 minutes] Speaker presentation + Q&A
  • [? minutes] Hangout or head to a nearby restaurant/pub for after presentation conversation.

Workshop

Presented by developers for developers to provide hands on experience with new technologies to help assist with the continuous professional growth demanded by our industry.

Agenda:

  • [15 minutes] Networking + eating refreshments (if provided).
  • [1.5+ hours] Speaker + Workshop + Q&A

Round Table Discussion

Usual held at lunch, attendees participate in a round table discussion with other experts to share past experiences on more advanced topics. The moderator/host should prepare some talking points on what you think is best practices and any (good or bad) experiences. We aim to connect companies to help each other by recommending any tips or strategies.

Agenda:

  • [15 minutes] Networking + eating refreshments (if provided).
  • [45 - 60 minutes] Round Table Discussion
